Our Divine Identity

Position Statement

I believe that each person is a spirit son or daughter of our Heavenly Parents. By recognizing my eternal identity it helps me find purpose in my life, and helps me make better choices and have a relationship with Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ.

Quotes

  • "Our theology begins with heavenly parents. Our highest aspiration is to be like them." — President Dallin H. Oaks
  • "You are a child of God. Spiritually, you are of noble birth, the offspring of the King of Heaven." — President Russell M. Nelson
  • "The happiness that is in your future will come from your capacity to recognize and feel how much God loves you." — President Henry B. Eyring
  • "You are created in the image of God and have inherited divine attributes." — Elder Jeffrey R. Holland
  • "The Spirit will testify of your divine worth as you seek Him." — Elder D. Todd Christofferson
  • "Understanding your divine identity brings peace and direction in a turbulent world." — President Russell M. Nelson
  • "The worth of souls is great in the sight of God." — President Russell M. Nelson
  • "You are known personally by God and have a unique purpose in His plan." — Elder Ronald A. Rasband
  • "You are a beloved child of Heavenly Father, born with infinite potential." — Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf
  • "We have a divine nature and destiny as children of heavenly parents." — Elder Dallin H. Oaks

The Power of Jesus Christ and Your Covenants

Position Statement

I believe in the power of Jesus Christ’s Atonement. The Atonement provides forgiveness, healing, and hope. As we make and keep sacred covenants, especially those made in the temple, I am bound more closely to the Savior. The covenants strengthen my testimony and prepare me to see Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ again.

Quotes

  • "Everything taught in the temple, through instruction and through the Spirit, increases our understanding of Jesus Christ." — President Russell M. Nelson
  • "The safest place to be spiritually is living inside your temple covenants!" — President Henry B. Eyring
  • "Through the power of the Atonement of Jesus Christ, all can be cleansed and made pure and whole again." — President Dallin H. Oaks
  • "Sacred covenants provide the pathway back to God’s presence." — Elder Quentin L. Cook
  • "Our covenants bind us to Him and give us godly power." — President Russell M. Nelson
  • "Temple work makes these exquisite blessings available to all of God’s children." — President Russell M. Nelson
  • "The power of Christ’s Atonement is unlocked by covenant keeping." — Elder Jeffrey R. Holland
  • "As you keep your covenants, He will magnify you and strengthen you." — President Russell M. Nelson
  • "Whatever the outcome, all will be well because of the temple." — President Henry B. Eyring
  • "Making and keeping sacred covenants yokes us to and with the Lord Jesus Christ. He will guide and strengthen us." — Elder David A. Bednar

  • "If we teach with love, we will teach with power and will be a blessing to all of God’s children." — President Henry B. Eyring

  • "Teaching truth requires humility and grace." — Elder Gerrit W. Gong
  • "Love is the motive that must undergird all our teaching." — Elder Jeffrey R. Holland
  • "Teaching simply allows us to share the saving doctrine early on." — President Henry B. Eyring
  • "Correct with compassion, teach with tenderness." — Elder Neil L. Andersen
  • "The Savior’s love is the example for all who teach His gospel." — Elder Quentin L. Cook
  • "When we teach with love rather than harshness, hearts can be touched, minds opened, and lives changed." — Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf
  • "Let love be the motive and kindness the method as you share gospel truths. Hearts are most receptive when truth is presented in the Savior’s way—with compassion, understanding, and genuine care." — Elder Quentin L. Cook

  • "Your faith grows as you choose to take the Savior’s yoke upon you." — President Henry B. Eyring
  • "If you desire a secure testimony, you must seek personal revelation through prayer and study." — President Dallin H. Oaks
  • "Search, ponder, and pray to know for yourself." — Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf

Marriage

  • "A good marriage does not require a perfect man or a perfect woman. It only requires a man and a woman committed to strive together toward perfection." - President Dallin H. Oaks
  • "In a temple marriage, you make a covenant that puts you on a path toward an eternal partnership of love and support, guided by the commandments of the Lord." - President Henry B. Eyring
  • "In marriage, kindness, patience, and a gentle voice can transform ordinary moments into sacred expressions of love, strengthening the bond you share." - Elder Jeffrey R. Holland
  • "Marriage is ordained of God. When a husband and wife are united in covenant and centered in Christ, the partnership becomes an indispensable source of faith, support, and eternal progression." - Elder D. Todd Christofferson
  • "A covenant marriage is foundational to God’s plan. As a husband and wife are sealed by proper authority and faithfully keep their covenants, they invite divine power into their lives and home." - Elder David A. Bednar
  • "In marriage, we learn to love as Christ loves—selflessly, patiently, and enduringly. We discover that our differences can become strengths as we serve, forgive, and lift each other toward heaven." - Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf
  • "A loving marriage between a man and a woman is one of the clearest reflections of heavenly love. Within it, husband and wife help each other follow Jesus Christ, nurture children in truth, and find everlasting happiness." - Elder Neil L. Andersen
  • "In a covenant marriage, as we keep our promises to God and to each other, our home becomes a sacred place of refuge. The unity and love we build there bless not only our family but everyone who enters." - Elder Gary E. Stevenson
  • "Strong marriages thrive on making the Savior the center. With Him as your guide, your marriage can become a refuge of peace and love, sustaining both husband and wife through life’s inevitable storms." - Elder Ronald A. Rasband

  • "Preparing children to face challenges with faith and integrity is one of the greatest gifts parents can give—anchoring them in gospel truth and righteousness." - Elder D. Todd Christofferson
  • "As you share stories of the Savior and bear witness of His love, your children will feel His influence and their testimonies will grow steadily and surely."
  • "Children are precious gifts entrusted to our care. We are accountable for nurturing their faith, protecting their innocence, and helping them know that they are loved by Heavenly Father." - Elder Jeffrey R. Holland
  • "Consistent family prayer, scripture study, and home-centered gospel learning create an environment in which children can develop their own testimonies and draw closer to the Savior." - Elder David A. Bednar
  • "As we help children feel the Savior’s love through kind words and Christlike service, we invite them to understand and embrace eternal truths with open hearts." - Elder Ulisses Soares
  • "By teaching our children about the Savior’s atoning love and guiding them toward righteous choices, we instill in them hope, faith, and the desire to follow Him throughout their lives." - Elder Dale G. Renlund
  • "When children know they belong to a loving, eternal family, they gain a sense of identity and purpose. They walk the covenant path with confidence, assured that Heavenly Father and the Savior walk it with them." - Elder Gerrit W. Gong

Service

Position Statement

I believe that serving others invites God’s love into our lives. By serving selflessly, I become more like the Savior and develop greater love for those around me. Service blesses both giver and receiver, fostering unity and faith.

Quotes

  • "In choosing to serve, we become the Lord’s hands, helping to heal hearts, lighten burdens, and spread joy—just as He did." - Elder Gary E. Stevenson
  • "Through willing service, we tap into divine love and power, and we become instruments in blessing the lives of others." - Elder Dale G. Renlund
  • "When we serve, we begin to see others as God sees them—precious souls to be nurtured, blessed, and uplifted." - Elder Gerrit W. Gong
  • "In dedicating our time and talents to serving others, our hearts are enlarged, our understanding deepens, and our lives become more Christlike." - Elder Ulisses Soares
  • "In lifting others through service, we not only ease their burdens but find that our own hearts grow lighter and our love for God and His children increases." - Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf
  • "A determining and refining test of true discipleship is how we care for the poor, the needy, and the marginalized. In serving them, we love and serve the Lord." - Elder Jeffrey R. Holland
  • "Service sanctifies both the giver and the receiver. By serving in the Lord’s way, we become His hands on the earth, learn to love as He loves, and draw nearer to Him." - Elder David A. Bednar
  • "As we serve God's children selflessly, even in small and simple ways, we witness miracles of healing and hope. Our humble efforts are magnified, and we grow in faith and discipleship." - Elder Neil L. Andersen
  • "When we offer timely and compassionate service to others, we serve God Himself. Our willingness to help His children is evidence that we keep our covenants and love Him." - President Henry B. Eyring

Personal Application Write-Up

As I worked on this practicum since the beginning of the semester, I tried to apply the principles I studied in my own life. I worked on strengthening my personal discipleship by improving my spiritual habits. I read the scriptures daily and offered more meaningful prayers. As I did this, I learned to rely on His Atonement and felt greater purpose in my life.

I also strove to “let God prevail” by listening to the words of the prophets and apostles. Each week, I would listen to conference talks and work on applying their teachings. I discussed these talks with my children and husband, and I found that as I learned more about each apostle, I came to love them more and saw their love for all of Heavenly Father’s children.

By sharing what I learned with my family, I improved my communication and fostered open dialogue about the gospel. Hearing my children express their understanding and testimonies was a joyful experience.

For my personal applications, I showed my husband more gratitude and encouragement, nurtured my children’s faith through consistent Come, Follow Me study at home, and served those in need. These efforts brought increased unity, love, and spiritual strength into my home and life. I truly felt the blessings of applying these principles as I served in church and among my family and neighbors.
